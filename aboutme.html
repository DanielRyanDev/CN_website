About Me...

Hello!


My name's Dan and welcome to this small chunk of webspace I call my own. I will be using this, mainly, as a way of showcasing my skills and portfolio. However, I have included some other things that may be of interest to you; the reader (or surfer, whatever you prefer.) Following the links, you will find my aforementioned portfolio; along with some stuff about who I am, where I'm from, and some of my interests and achievements outside of computing.

The Journey Begins...

So far, I've had a few achievements throughout my life - some a bit stranger than others. For instance, way back in 2012, I was approached in the middle of Liverpool City Centre by a black-clad lad asking if I wanted to take part in Juice FM's 'Street Style Awards'. Never one to miss an opportunity (nor have the confidence to say 'no' to a stranger), I took up the offer... imagine my surprise when I won it! The awards night was a red carpet affair; celebrities you or I have never heard of and the finest furniture Wayfair had to offer adorned the beautiful innards of Liverpool Cathedral. Fortunately, for me, I had met a girl a few weeks before; looking to impress, I asked her to be my plus one. Nine years later, we're still here together. If she'd have said no - I was to take my mum. Unfortunately, the high-society life of a catalogue model could not deter the allure of fried chicken and pints of Mild. The pounds increased - just not the right kind and I headed off to college and then onto University.


The Broth Begins To Boil...

Around this time, I managed to snag my very first dedicated gaming PC (the benefits of turning 18.) I'd always been a gamer; a nerd, a lover of all things fantasy, sci-fi, and tech - I'd never, up to this point, thought that it could be something that someone like me would be able to pursue. After I finished my Music Btec in college, I took a chance and applied to Liverpool Hope University's new Computer Science degree. Months of waiting with abated breath passed and finally, on my 20th Birthday, a letter came through the door - I had been accepted! In a scene reminiscent of Charlie Bucket finding his Golden Ticket, I skipped to my partner's house to give her the good news - along with some birthday cake, naturally.


'Smart Lad, To Slip Betimes Away...'

Okay, let's cut to the chase: the course wasn't all it cracked up to be. I was expecting to be getting my hands dirty; mastering my craft, cracking problems, conjuring solutions! As Thomas Edison once said: "There's a way to do it better - find it!"... yeah, it didn't happen like that. Instead, I was stuck in a gloomy lecture hall; watching endless lecturers write for statements and while loops, wishing I could be in front of a monitor learning this rather than noting them down in a notepad - only to run into issues with the code later and no clear way of resolving problems. In the end, I switched courses and pursued History and Politics - I had failed. I felt defeated and soon, gray clouds would hang for, what felt like, forever. I got my head down; resigned to the reality that I was destined to be a paper-pusher for the rest of my days. I convinced myself I'd made the right decision - that computers and their whims would never had led to anything for me... quite an odd premise when you consider I'd come home and revel in the (often self-inflicted) challenges that various hardware and software would conjure...

Coronavirus (Or How I Learned To Stop Worrying And Love Lockdown)

Anyway, I graduated in January 2020; attended my ceremony (after much persuasion from loved ones), ate a meal, toasted with friends, and went home - not feeling fulfilled very much; an underwhelming affair, by any estimate. Then Coronavirus happened. Then I entered employment. Then everything changed. Being stuck in a dead-end job made me sit up; all that sitting at home not able to go anywhere; stewing away and thinking rather than doing. Honestly? I began to get on my own nerves with my sitting around waiting for something to happene rather than making something happen; I left my job and pursued a Comptia A+ course. At the time of writing, I'm weeks away from my exam - wish me luck folks!

The Present & The Future

That's how I ended up here - writing this overtly-dramatic biography. I have been on every computer course, programme, and, bootcamp I could find - but I hadn't managed to get rid of the coding bug. Enter: CodeNation. I saw that CodeNation were running a three-week course to help get started in the world of coding - I have a cursory knowledge from my year of study and general interest, so why not? The past three weeks, I have learned so much; felt so much better in myself and truly believe I am doing something that is important. I feel fulfilled. Hopefully, I can kick-off from here and launch my career in coding. To paraphrase Charles Dederic: Today is the beginning of the rest of my life. So, as I sit here typing this on my last day of CodeNation, I hope you've found something of worth and, more importantly, humour in my pretentious soliloquy. Anyways, back to trying to figure out why Flexbox isn't working folks! TTFN!

Daniel Ryan (3rd September 2021)
